Don Quixote

by Miguel de Cervantes

"When life itself seems lunatic, who knows where madness lies? Perhaps to be too practical is madness. To surrender dreams - this may be madness. Too much sanity may be madness - and maddest of all: to see life as it is, and not as it should be!"

Part 1, Chapter 26

Don Quixote spends this whole chapter stomping around in the woods, utterly devastated. All the while he is commending himself to God and writing depressing poetry. Sancho Panza reveals to some gentlemen the extent of his own insanity brought about by his master's wild delusions.

Part 2, Chapter 12

In this chapter we read of Don Quixote and Sancho Panza making camp in the woods for the night. There is a brief respite in Don Quixote's madness and a rambling yet heartfelt dialogue between the two takes place that exemplifies the strong bond they have.
